## 3. Calibration drift signals

### Signal 1 — Severity always-4 pattern
**Observation**: Across 5 scorecards, the Severity dimension is scored 4 for nearly every agent that calibrated severity correctly. A 5 in Severity is reserved for "perfectly calibrated AND surfaced a real production risk."

**Risk**: This creates a soft cap that prevents true 35/35 scores from emerging organically for non-gap-hunter agents. It also means Severity ceases to be a discriminating dimension across most agents.

**Recommendation**: Allow Severity 5 when the agent both (a) calibrated correctly and (b) explained the calibration reasoning explicitly. Don't reserve 5 for "surfaced production risk" — that's Coverage's job.

### Signal 2 — gap-hunter perfect-score streak
**Observation**: 4 consecutive 35/35 scores. While gap-hunter genuinely is a high-performer, perfect-score streaks invite confirmation bias.

**Risk**: Observer may stop probing for weak dimensions on gap-hunter specifically.

**Recommendation**: On the next gap-hunter verdict, deliberately identify at least one dimension where a 4 would be defensible. If genuinely none is defensible, retain 35 — but force the test.

### Signal 3 — 94% EXEMPLARY rate
**Observation**: Verdict-tier distribution skews heavily toward EXEMPLARY.

**Risk**: Either (a) the fleet is genuinely exemplary (plausible given GATE machinery), or (b) the threshold of 28/35 for EXEMPLARY is too low for the actual score distribution this observer produces.

**Recommendation**: Not a threshold change (the rubric is the rubric). Instead, sharpen the upper end — distinguish "EXEMPLARY-baseline (28-30)" from "EXEMPLARY-standout (31-35)" in commentary. The verdict tier stays the same; the per-agent notes do the work of distinguishing.

### Signal 4 — Cross-scorecard agent identity stability
**Observation**: Same agents appear across most campaigns (system-architect, gap-hunter, adr-historian, final-consistency-review). This makes longitudinal comparison meaningful but also means observer is repeatedly scoring the same agents under similar conditions.

**Risk**: Pattern-fitting — observer may unconsciously anchor to prior scores rather than scoring independently.

**Recommendation**: Score the verdict block first, THEN compare to prior. Currently the cross-reference is done as part of the scoring; separating the two phases would reduce anchoring.
